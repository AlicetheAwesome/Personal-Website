Deep Learning for Ultrasonic Breast Tumor Image Segmentation

Ultrasonic Breast Tumor images (left); Doctor Diagnosis of tumor contour (center); Deep Learning Prediction of tumor contour(right)

Abstract

Breast cancer is the most invasive cancer in women, however, it requires a lot of time and experience for radiologists to diagnose an ultrasonic breast tumor image. Thus, in this research, we propose to use semantic segmentation methods to assist doctors diagnose an ultrasound breast tumor image. We used the original ultrasonic breast tumor image as our input, the segmented image as the ground truth, and built our model based on the U-Net model. We compared our modelâ€™s accuracy with Fully Convolutional Networks (FCN), and analyzed the effects of applying median blur to images before training. According to our results, the U-Net model can achieve an accuracy of 80.58%, exceeding the 66.74% accuracy of Fully Convolutional Networks (FCN). However, applying median blur to the images for noise reduction cannot increase the accuracy of our model. Overall, the U-Net model can accurately predict the contour of the tumor.
