Deep Learning for Ultrasonic Breast Tumor Image Segmentation

Ultrasonic Breast Tumor images (left); Doctor Diagnosis of tumor contour (center); Deep Learning Prediction of tumor contour(right)

Abstract

Breast cancer is the most invasive cancer in women. However, it requires a lot of time and experience for radiologists to diagnose an ultrasound breast tumor image. Thus, in this research, we propose to use semantic segmentation methods to assist doctors diagnose an ultrasound breast tumor image.

We used the original ultrasound breast tumor images as our input, the segmented images as the ground truth, and built our machine learning model based on U-Net. We then compared our modelâ€™s accuracy with the FCN (Fully Convolutional Network) model. In addition, we applied median blur image processing before training to access its effect on our model's accuracy.

According to our results, the our model can achieve an accuracy of 80.58%, exceeding the 66.74% accuracy of the FCN model. However, applying median blur to the images for noise reduction cannot increase the accuracy of our model. Overall, the U-Net model can accurately predict the contour of the tumor.

The Germs in Belly Button Lint

photographed by differential interferance contrast (DIC) microscopy

Abstract

Nowadays, keeping ourselves clean has become a very important issue. However, often times people ignore the lint accumalating in their belly button, allowing a variety of germs to cultivate inside the hole. In this project, we cultivate the germs and sequenced their DNA, and found two types of germs that are most dominant in the belly button, Staphylococcus hominis and Micrococcus genus .
